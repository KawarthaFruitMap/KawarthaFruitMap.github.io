How This Will Work

Kawartha and Haliburton Community Fruit Tree and Vegetable Harvesting and Sharing program

Register

Harvest

Share

Are you an in-town or rural landowner that has fruit trees that produce edible fruit?

  • Register

    If so, you can join us by registering here…(link to form)

    Your contact and tree information will be added to a map that will only be seen/available by these local food program partners,,,,list here.

  • Harvest

    Staff from these programs could contact you in the fall to arrange a time that is convenient for you for volunteer harvesters to come to your home and pick the fruit

  • Share

    The harvest from each fruit pick is split 3 ways: ⅓ is provided to the landowner, ⅓ is split amongst the picking volunteers, and ⅓ will be delivered by the volunteers to the food program. This food program will share your fruit with seniors, adults and children in your community that are hungry, live in poverty and are in need

Are you interested in becoming a volunteer gleaner/picker/harvester

If so, please register with us here…(link to form) Your information will like landowners also only be available to our food program partners. They could contact you at harvest time to request your help to pick/harvest/glean fruit and or vegetables to share with the landowner, yourself and to deliver the 1/3 harvest to the food program

Providing Food

-providing access to healthy food for children, seniors and adults who live with hunger and in food poverty in our communities, each tree providing as much as 15 bushes per year
(link to Health Unit or KLFS statistics)
-providing an opportunity to the public to be reconnected to food trees, become more aware, supportive and engaged in local urban ecology and local food.
-increasing public awareness on local poverty and provide an opportunity to become engaged in effective community action through acts of kindness for themselves and others, come together and have a direct effect on the health of our community
