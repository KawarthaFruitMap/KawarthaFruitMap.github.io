Are you an in-town or rural landowner that has fruit trees that produce edible fruit?
Are you a gardener who has extra vegetables at the end of the season?

  • Register

    If so, you can join us by registering here:

    Your contact and tree or available vegetable information will be added to a map that will only be seen/available by our local food program partners

  • Harvest

    Staff from these programs could contact you in the fall to arrange a time that is convenient for you for volunteer harvesters to come to your home and pick the fruit or vegetables

  • Share

    The harvest from each fruit pick is split 3 ways: ⅓ is provided to the landowner, ⅓ is split amongst the picking volunteers, and ⅓ will be delivered by the volunteers to the food program. This food program will share your fruit with seniors, adults and children in your community that are hungry, live in poverty and are in need

Providing Food

• Providing access to healthy food for children, seniors and adults who live with hunger and in food poverty in our communities (each tree providing as much as 15 bushes per year).
• Providing an opportunity for the public to be reconnected to food trees, become more aware, supportive and engaged in local urban ecology and local food.
• Increasing public awareness on local poverty and provide an opportunity to become engaged in effective community action through acts of kindness for themselves and others, come together and have a direct effect on the health of our community.

Supporting Wildlife

• Decreasing food waste.
• Accessing local food supply to replace a portion of our emission-intense global food supply.
• Acting as an important addition to the City of Kawartha Lakes Bee City pollination map to help identify gaps in the current pollinator pathway and inform future forestry planning.

Build Community

• Connecting people to nature, teaching them tree ecology and inspiring them to adopt a more environmentally sustainable life.
• Reminding & teaching people the value of trees and green spaces, encouraging them to become better stewards of our urban environment.
• Increasing social inclusion & participant's health by engaging with others and connecting with neighbours through hands-on effective community actions.
• Increasing public awareness that our local food banks and food programs accept healthy fruit and vegetables through locally established programs.
